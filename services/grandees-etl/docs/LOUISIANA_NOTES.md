# Louisiana Adjudicated Property — Key Differences from Texas

## How Louisiana Works
Louisiana does NOT run a standard tax lien certificate or tax deed auction like Texas.
Instead, properties that go delinquent for multiple years become **adjudicated** —
the parish takes title via a court judgment. These are then sold by the parish.

## Acquisition Path
1. Property goes delinquent → parish files suit → court issues judgment → property adjudicated to parish
2. Parish maintains an adjudicated property roll (public record)
3. Parish Council approves sales individually OR holds bulk sales
4. Buyer gets a deed directly from the parish — no redemption period after completed sale
5. Title insurance IS recommended — prior mortgages and liens may survive (unlike tax deeds in some states)

## St. Charles Parish Process
- Adjudicated property sales are approved by the Parish Council
- Current list: https://www.stcharlesgov.net
- Contact: Parish Government, Hahnville, LA
- Priority targets: commercial parcels, vacant land, distressed residential

## Key Advantage
- No redemption period once sale is complete (unlike Texas 2-year window)
- Prices often well below market — minimal investor competition vs. Houston market
- Jefferson Parish volume is very high — good deal flow for West Bank targets
