# Unclaimed Property Market Analysis - Texas & Houston Metro

## Executive Summary

This comprehensive market analysis covers unclaimed property recovery in Texas and the Houston metropolitan area for the past five fiscal years, providing critical data on market size, competitor landscape, and fee benchmarking for regulatory and business planning purposes.

## 5. FEE BENCHMARKING & LEGAL CAVEATS

### Texas Legal Framework

**Statutory Maximum Fee (Texas Property Code § 72.305):**
- **Maximum Legal Fee:** 10% of recovered value
- **Enforcement:** Non-compliant fees are unenforceable in Texas
- **Disclosure Requirement:** Must be disclosed in writing (Finder Agreement)
- **Application:** Applies to all search firms and private investigators

### Industry Fee Ranges

| Service Type | Fee Range | Model | Disclosure Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Simple Individual Claim** | 10-15% | Contingent | Finder Agreement |
| **Estate/Probate Claim** | 15-35% | Contingent | Written agreement + probate court filing |
| **Multi-state Claim** | 15-30% | Contingent | Engagement letter |
| **High-Value Corporate** | 10-25% | Contingent or flat rate | Engagement letter |
| **Complex International** | 20-40% | Contingent | Detailed service agreement |
| **Government Program** | FREE | Direct access | N/A |

### Fee Disclosure Best Practices

**Required Disclosures:**
1. **Finder Agreement (signed):** Outlines fee percentage, scope of services, contingency terms
2. **Fee Calculation Method:** Gross recovery or net recovery basis
3. **Expense Coverage:** Whether finder covers costs (notarization, filing fees, etc.)
4. **Timeline:** Estimated processing time
5. **State-Specific Caps:** In Texas, capped at 10%; other states may vary

**Sample Finder Agreement Elements:**
- Party identification (client and finder)
- Scope of services (search, claim filing, follow-up)
- Fee structure (percentage of recovery)
- Payment timing (upon receipt of funds)
- Confidentiality clause
- Dispute resolution method
- Signature and date

## 6. PUBLIC COMPLAINTS & BBB REVIEW SUMMARY

### Common Complaint Categories

**Issue: Fee Surprise**
- Clients report unexpected additional fees deducted from recovered funds
- **Root Cause:** Unclear upfront disclosure
- **Legal Caveat:** All fees must be disclosed in writing in advance

**Issue: Lack of Communication**
- Clients report difficulty reaching service providers during claim process
- **Best Practice:** Regular status updates via email/phone

**Issue: Unnecessary Services**
- Some finders contact clients about funds already publicly available
- **Market Reality:** Finder value is convenience, not access (official databases are free)

**Issue: Non-Performance**
- Some finders fail to actually locate funds or complete claims
- **Legal Recourse:** BBB complaints, attorney general, small claims court

## 8. REGULATORY & LEGAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Licensing & Compliance

**Texas Requirements:**
- Some recovery firms regulated by Texas Department of Public Safety
- Voluntary Better Business Bureau accreditation available
- Finder agreements must comply with Texas Property Code Chapter 72
- Fee caps strictly enforced by state attorney general

**Multi-State Considerations:**
- Fee caps vary by state (5-15% typical range)
- Some states require registration before operating
- Disclosure requirements differ across jurisdictions
- NAUPA provides state-by-state regulatory guidance

### Litigation Risk

**Common Dispute Triggers:**
1. Fee disputes (most common)
2. Undisclosed expenses
3. Lack of results despite promising contact
4. Unauthorized charges to credit cards
5. Multiple fees for same claim

**Legal Caveats for Service Providers:**
- Clear written disclosure of all fees
- No upfront payment collection (contingency basis required in most states)
- Compliance with state-specific regulations
- Professional liability insurance recommended
- Proper record-keeping and audit trails
