# Investigator Agreement (Draft)

This Investigator Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of the Effective Date set forth in the Finder Agreement between the claimant and Adriatic Claim Co (the "Company"). This draft is for internal use and counsel review — do not rely on it as legal advice.

Parties
- Company: Adriatic Claim Co (Owner: [Owner name — Wife])
- Investigator: Dejuan [Last name] ("Investigator")

Recitals
- The Company operates a finder service for locating unclaimed funds and assisting claimants in recovering those funds.
- The Investigator will perform investigative and discovery services on behalf of the Company and its claimants, subject to licensing, bonding, and applicable law.

1. Scope of Services
- The Investigator will perform the following services as directed by the Company: name-based searches, records checks, outreach to potential claimants, collection of supporting documents, observational investigations where permitted, and preparing claim-ready packages for filing with governmental entities.
- The Investigator will not provide legal advice or represent claimants in court. If legal representation is necessary, the Company will refer claimants to a licensed attorney.

2. License & Compliance
- The Investigator represents and warrants that he holds all necessary licenses, registrations, and bonds required to perform investigator activities in the jurisdictions where services are performed.
- Investigator must provide copies of all applicable licenses and any required bond or insurance certificates to the Company prior to commencing work. Investigator agrees to promptly notify the Company if any license, bond, or insurance is revoked, suspended, or otherwise impaired.

3. Employment Status & Compensation
- The Investigator will act as [select: independent contractor / employee] — to be determined and documented by the Company before work commences. The Company will provide appropriate tax forms and compensation structure consistent with that classification.
- Compensation: Investigator will receive compensation as set forth in a separate Schedule A (commission, hourly rate, or salary). Any fee splits or equity arrangements must be documented in writing.

4. Confidentiality & Data Protection
- Investigator will only access claimant PII on a need-to-know basis and must follow Company data handling policies (RBAC, encryption, secure transfer). Investigator will not export, share, or sell claimant PII without explicit, documented claimant consent and Company approval.
- Investigator will comply with all applicable data protection laws and Company security policies.

5. Consent & Representations
- Investigator acknowledges that claimant consent (via Finder Agreement and signed POA where required) is required before the Investigator may act on a claimant's behalf or share their data with third parties. The Company will ensure consent language includes permission to perform the investigative tasks described herein and to share results with partners where consented.

6. Insurance & Indemnity
- Investigator must maintain appropriate insurance while performing services (professional liability/E&O, general liability, and fidelity/bonding if handling funds). The Company may require proof of coverage.
- Investigator agrees to indemnify and hold harmless the Company against claims arising from Investigator's willful misconduct or gross negligence.

7. Term & Termination
- This Agreement continues until terminated by either party with [30] days' written notice. The Company may immediately suspend Investigator access for regulatory or compliance concerns.

8. Governing Law
- This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Texas.

9. Counsel Review Required
- This draft requires review and modification by a Texas-licensed attorney prior to use.
