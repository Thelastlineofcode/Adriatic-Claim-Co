### Texas-Specific Regulations

**Texas Property Code § 72.305 (Maximum Fee Provision):**
- **Maximum Fee Enforceable:** 10% of recovered unclaimed property
- **Fee Above 10%:** Unenforceable in Texas (client can refuse to pay)
- **Application:** Applies to all search firms and private investigators
- **Enforcement:** Texas Attorney General office

**Disclosure Requirements:**
- Written Finder Agreement required
- Must specify fee percentage clearly
- Must disclose contingent nature (no recovery = no fee)
- Must state estimated timeline
- Client signature required

**Prohibited Practices:**
- Upfront payments (contingency required)
- Misrepresenting claim value
- Charging "processing fees" outside contingency
- Unauthorized charges to credit cards

### Fee Dispute Resolution in Texas

**Complaint Process:**
1. Contact the finder company directly (attempt resolution)
2. File complaint with Better Business Bureau (if applicable)
3. Report to Texas Attorney General Consumer Protection Division
4. Small Claims Court filing (if amount < $20,000)
5. Arbitration clause in agreement (if included)

### Top Complaints Analysis

**High Frequency: Fee Surprise (45% of complaints)**
- Cause: Unclear upfront disclosure; additional unexpected fees
- Example: Finder deducts $500 from $5,000 recovery without explicit pre-disclosure
- Resolution: Fee renegotiation or arbitration
- Prevention: Detailed written Finder Agreement signed before work begins

**Moderate Frequency: Lack of Communication (30%)**
- Cause: Overwhelmed finders with high caseload
- Example: Client can't reach finder for weeks during claim process
- Resolution: Management escalation + regular email/phone updates
- Prevention: Establish communication SLA in agreement

**Moderate Frequency: Excessive Fees vs. Work (15%)**
- Cause: Misunderstanding of contingency model
- Example: Finder claims $1,000 fee for minimal work (just form submission)
- Resolution: Fee reduction negotiation
- Prevention: Clear explanation that convenience fee justifies contingency

- **Premium Rate:** 15-35% for complex/estate claims (justified by complexity)

### Regulatory Compliance Critical Success Factors
1. **Clear Disclosure:** Written Finder Agreement with specific fee percentage
2. **Texas Compliance:** Maximum 10% fee in Texas (enforceable only up to this)
3. **Licensing:** Consider Texas DPS licensing for credibility

5. **Communication:** Regular status updates prevent complaints
